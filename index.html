In a nutshell

I am a machine learning graduate with an MSc in Artificial Intelligence(Merit) from the University of St Andrews and BSc in Computing with Management(First) from the University of Leicester. I gained professional programming experience as a developer at ISIS Neutron and Muon Source of Science and Technology Facilities Council and while teaching at the University of Leicester.

I have experience with multiple machine learning technologies, such as TensorFlow/Keras, scikit-learn and NLTK, as well as statistical analysis tools like pandas and numpy. Apart from Python, which I usually use for machine learning projects, I am also proficient with Objective C++, which was my first programming language and which, beside Python, I used heavily during my professional time at ISIS.

I learned to be a part of a multi national team with great code development practices, extensive git collaboration, stand-up meetings(oftentimes remote) and continuous integration. I also learned to communicate my ideas clearly, what got me a job at academic teaching.

Over time I realised that what gives me the biggest satisfaction is asking difficult, perhaps unobvious questions and seeking answers. Constant element of research is why I chose artificial intelligence in the first place, so I stay up to date with AI news, including some academic works, like Minds and Machines.
