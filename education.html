Education

Over the years I have been attending different schools in my home city to eventually come for my studies in the United Kingdom.

For the most of my life I was a student in two schools at the same time - general education school and music school. In Poland in order to learn music one has to attend a school dedicated to teaching music. Mathematics, English, physics etc. during the day, harmony, instruments aural training and music history in the evening. On top of that any homework and daily instrument practice.

In high school I started my programming adventure with C++. I enjoyed it a lot and decided to change my focus from physics to computer science and took it on my Matura exams. It is a test taken at the end of high school, an equivalent of A-levels, although from long talks with British students I know it is difficult to compare the two reliably. Apart from various compulsory tests in mathematics and Polish language, students have to choose at least one subject to take on extended level. I took extended computer science, mathematics and English language.

After finishing the music school and obtaining a diploma I moved to England to study at University of Leicester.
